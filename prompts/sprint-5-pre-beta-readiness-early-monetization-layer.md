# Prompt — Sprint 5 Pre-Beta Readiness & Early Monetization Layer

Continue Lynknov after Sprint 4.

## Context
Sprint 0–4 are complete.
Lynknov is now a founder-tested MVP.
We are preparing for a limited early beta / trusted user exposure.

## Main goal of Sprint 5
Make Lynknov feel operationally ready for pre-beta and introduce a very light monetization-aware structure without overbuilding.

## Strategic frame
Lynknov is not just a page builder.
It is an Interactive Business OS.
However, at this stage we still protect MVP focus.

## Build priorities

### A. Pre-beta readiness
Improve:
- route consistency
- navigation consistency
- form reliability
- error handling
- loading handling
- no-data states
- baseline mobile quality
- baseline accessibility improvements if practical

### B. Owner experience improvements
Add or improve:
- lead status workflow clarity
- recent activity usefulness
- stronger publish confidence
- page share actions if practical
- lightweight settings clarity

### C. Monetization-aware foundation
Still do NOT add real billing.
But you may implement:
- account plan field or subscription status field if needed
- feature gating helpers
- simple UI placeholders for future premium features
- upgrade messaging placeholders only if tasteful

Keep this subtle and non-intrusive.

### D. Light growth readiness
Prepare the app for future beta users:
- clearer onboarding copy
- clearer call-to-action language
- better trust-building empty states
- founder-friendly sample content or starter defaults if practical

### E. Release readiness
Create or update:
- MVP release checklist
- known issues list
- internal QA checklist
- deployment sanity checklist
- environment requirements summary

## Important constraints
- no real payment checkout yet
- no team collaboration yet
- no complex analytics suite yet
- no marketplace yet
- no AI writing suite yet
- no massive scope increase

## Product quality bar
The product should feel:
- focused
- founder-useful
- credible
- premium enough
- stable enough for limited trusted usage

## Output format
Return:
1. route/file changes
2. key product improvements
3. monetization foundation added
4. release readiness docs/checklists created
5. QA notes
6. known gaps before external beta
7. recommendation for Sprint 6

## Success criteria
At the end of Sprint 5:
- Lynknov feels ready for trusted pre-beta usage
- the founder can confidently show and use it
- the product has the beginnings of monetization structure
- the repo and UX feel much more mature without losing MVP discipline
