# 1. Product framing before design

Before any UI is generated, the agent must understand what Lynknov is
trying to become. The landing page is not only a marketing surface; it
is the first expression of product identity.

-   Lynknov is positioned as an Interactive Business OS for creators,
    > freelancers, consultants, service businesses, and modern small
    > brands.

-   Its purpose is to transform a digital presence into a structured
    > system for presentation, lead capture, conversion, and growth.

-   The page must communicate system thinking, calm confidence, and
    > premium product quality instead of loud sales energy.

# 2. What this blueprint is for

-   Translate high-level brand direction into a concrete landing page
    > structure.

-   Help ChatGPT and Windsurf generate sections that are aligned with
    > product positioning.

-   Provide clear acceptance criteria so later evaluation and refactor
    > are easier.

-   Act as internal documentation for future team members or
    > collaborators.

## Typography recommendation:

-   Headings: Inter Tight (or a similar crisp display sans).

-   Body: Inter for readability and product-grade neutrality.

-   Hero typography should be large, bold or semibold, and slightly
    > tighter in tracking.

-   Section labels can use smaller uppercase text with subtle tracking
    > to create an enterprise-grade rhythm.

# 6. Motion and interaction rules

-   Use subtle fade and upward reveal, not heavy parallax or flashy
    > loops.

-   Hover states should feel precise: slight lift, border emphasis, and
    > soft glow.

-   Sticky navbar may gain blur and stronger border as the user scrolls.

-   Buttons can use a restrained gradient and minimal scale change, but
    > avoid playful motion.

# 8. Section-by-section blueprint

## Navbar

Keep it sparse and premium. Use only essential navigation: Product,
Solutions or Use Cases, Login, and a primary CTA such as Get Started.

-   Logo should be simple and wordmark-led.

-   Navbar should feel transparent or lightly glassed until scroll.

-   Primary CTA should not compete with the hero, only support it.

## Hero

This is the most important section. It must state that Lynknov turns a
digital presence into a growth system, not just a prettier page.

-   Large headline with high confidence.

-   One supporting paragraph, not three.

-   Primary CTA plus a softer secondary CTA.

-   Product preview should feel like real software, not a stock
    > illustration.

## Problem framing

Explain why most digital presence tools stop at presentation. They look
decent but do not structure lead capture, conversion, or growth
workflows.

-   Keep problem statements concise and credible.

-   Avoid fear-heavy or manipulative copy.

-   Visually, a clean 2-column or card comparison works well.

## Why Lynknov

Present the 3-4 differentiators that justify the product in simple
terms.

-   Structured Presence

-   Lead Capture Layer

-   Conversion Flow

-   Business Identity System

Continue the remaining sections with the same discipline: short
explanation, deliberate hierarchy, and a visual treatment that feels
more like a premium operating layer than a marketing collage.

intelligent. It should never feel like a hype-driven template SaaS site.

## Suggested hero headline options:

-   Turn Your Presence Into a Growth System

-   More Than a Landing Page. A Business Operating Layer.

-   Build a Smarter Digital Presence for Growth

-   The Interactive Business OS for Modern Brands and Service Businesses

## Suggested subheadline direction:

-   Lynknov helps creators, freelancers, consultants, and modern
    > businesses structure their digital presence to present clearly,
    > capture interest, and convert better.

-   Built for people who need more than a simple bio page - Lynknov
    > brings identity, lead flow, and conversion logic into one premium
    > system.

# 10. Visual implementation notes for Windsurf or other coding agents

-   Use dark mode by default with a matte black-navy base.

-   Keep generous vertical spacing between sections and do not overload
    > a single fold.

-   Prefer one strong product mockup over many small decorative visuals.

-   Use subtle blue-violet gradients only on accents, CTA surfaces, and
    > glows.

-   Do not overuse glassmorphism, neon effects, or overly futuristic
    > motifs.

-   Use rounded cards with thin borders and soft elevation.

-   Animations should be subtle and should never become the main
    > attraction.

# 11. Prompt template for the landing page build

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------
  Design and implement a premium dark-mode landing page for Lynknov, an
  Interactive Business OS for creators, freelancers, consultants, service
  businesses, and modern small brands. The page should feel clean,
  minimal, strategic, and product-grade - closer to Apple / Linear /
  modern premium SaaS than to a generic startup template. Use the
  following section order: navbar, hero, problem framing, why Lynknov,
  core system, use cases, dashboard preview, trust layer, final CTA,
  footer. Use restrained indigo-violet accents, large typography,
  generous spacing, subtle motion, and realistic product mockups. Avoid
  neon, heavy glassmorphism, visual clutter, and hype-driven copy.

# 12. Acceptance criteria for review

-   A first-time visitor can understand what Lynknov is within the hero
    > section.

-   The page does not feel like a link-in-bio template or generic AI
    > startup page.

-   The design feels premium, dark, calm, and scalable.

-   Every section earns its place and has a clear narrative role.

-   The CTA is visible, justified, and repeated without becoming noisy.

-   The product preview looks credible enough to support future in-app
    > design language.
