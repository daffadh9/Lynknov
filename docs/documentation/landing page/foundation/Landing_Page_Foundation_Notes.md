1\. Why the agent should understand Lynknov first

Before asking Windsurf or any coding agent to build the landing page,
the tool should receive a concise but clear brief about what Lynknov
actually is, who it serves, and what kind of product feeling it must
project. Without that context, the agent may produce a page that looks
polished on the surface but misses the actual positioning, message
hierarchy, and strategic tone of the platform.

Why this matters

> **•** A good landing page is not only a UI exercise. It is a
> translation of product strategy into visual hierarchy, copy, and
> conversion flow.
>
> **•** If the agent only knows that Lynknov is a startup website, it
> may default to a generic SaaS page with broad claims and mismatched
> sections.
>
> **•** If the agent understands that Lynknov is positioned as an
> Interactive Business OS, it will make better decisions about
> terminology, structure, design restraint, and the overall premium
> feel.

What the agent needs to understand

> **•** Lynknov is not just another bio-link tool or simple page
> builder.
>
> **•** Lynknov is intended to become a structured business presence and
> growth layer for modern creators, freelancers, consultants, agencies,
> and small businesses.
>
> **•** The page should communicate system thinking: presence, capture,
> conversion, and growth, not just a collection of disconnected
> features.
>
> **•** The product tone should feel premium, intelligent, calm, and
> serious, rather than loud, flashy, or overly salesy.

+-----------------------------------------------------------------------+
| **Practical rule**                                                    |
|                                                                       |
| Give the agent the product context first, then the landing page       |
| objective, then the exact section scope. This will produce more       |
| relevant copy, better UI choices, and less generic output.            |
+=======================================================================+

2\. Recommended context brief for Windsurf or another agent

Below is the kind of briefing summary that should be handed to the
coding or design agent before implementation starts.

  -------------------- --------------------------------------------------
  **Product**          Lynknov is an Interactive Business OS, not merely
                       a landing page builder or bio-link tool.

  **Core idea**        It helps users turn their digital presence into a
                       structured growth system.

  **Audience**         Modern creators, freelancers, consultants,
                       agencies, and small businesses that want a serious
                       and scalable digital presence.

  **Page objective**   Communicate positioning clearly, establish trust,
                       and move users toward sign-up or getting started.

  **Design tone**      Dark, premium, minimal, modern, calm, and
                       product-led. Think clean product company rather
                       than loud marketing page.

  **Content tone**     Strategic, concise, credible, and confident. Avoid
                       hype-heavy or generic SaaS wording.

  **Visual feel**      Apple-like cleanliness with dark-mode elegance,
                       subtle blue-violet accents, refined spacing, and
                       careful motion.
  -------------------- --------------------------------------------------

4\. Visual style and experience principles

The landing page should feel complete and credible without becoming
dense or visually noisy. The best reference mindset is not to imitate
Apple directly, but to borrow its clarity, restraint, and visual
confidence.

Key visual principles

> **•** Large, clean typography with strong hierarchy and generous
> spacing.
>
> **•** Dark mode surfaces with elegant contrast and carefully used
> accent color.
>
> **•** Minimal but complete structure: enough information to persuade,
> without overwhelming the visitor.
>
> **•** Subtle visual depth through soft gradients, light glow, layered
> cards, and high-quality product mockups.
>
> **•** Motion used as polish, not spectacle.

What to avoid

> **•** Overly bright neon gradients or heavy cyber aesthetics.
>
> **•** Too many sections with similar weight and no clear hierarchy.
>
> **•** Dense text blocks that make the page feel tiring to scan.
>
> **•** Generic AI or SaaS buzzwords that dilute Lynknov\'s identity.
>
> **•** Flashy motion, large parallax effects, or gimmicky animated
> backgrounds.

Operational advice

> **•** Do not ask the agent to build the entire product at once. Work
> one feature or one page layer at a time.
>
> **•** Keep the first implementation goal narrow: landing page only,
> then auth flow, then dashboard shell.
>
> **•** Always evaluate the result against product meaning, not just
> visual attractiveness.
>
> **•** Use refactoring tools after exploration, not before.
