# Lynknov Team Roles and Hiring Plan

## Purpose

This document defines the initial team structure Lynknov may grow into as the product becomes more serious and collaborative.

The goal is not to hire a large team immediately, but to clarify which roles are most valuable and in what order they would matter.

---

## Hiring Philosophy

Lynknov should prioritize hiring people who strengthen one or more of these areas:
- product clarity,
- frontend quality,
- system maintainability,
- user experience,
- and execution discipline.

Early hires should increase signal, not just output volume.

---

## Recommended Role Sequence

### Stage 1 — Founder-led with selective support
Primary mode:
- founder drives product, documentation, and direction
- limited specialist support used where needed

Useful support roles:
- frontend developer
- UI designer or design partner
- technical reviewer or senior engineering advisor

### Stage 2 — Core product team
As the product matures, the most useful core team structure is likely:
- founder / product lead
- frontend engineer
- full-stack engineer
- product designer
- growth or content operator

### Stage 3 — Expansion support
Later-stage roles may include:
- data or analytics specialist
- operations support
- customer success or onboarding support
- automation or systems specialist

---

## Role Definitions

### Founder / Product Lead
Owns:
- vision
- positioning
- product direction
- scope decisions
- documentation quality
- team alignment

### Frontend Engineer
Owns:
- interface implementation
- component quality
- interaction polish
- dashboard and landing consistency

### Full-Stack Engineer
Owns:
- backend integration
- data flow
- auth systems
- service logic
- performance and maintainability

### Product Designer
Owns:
- interface systems
- layout clarity
- product experience consistency
- UX direction and visual refinement

### Growth / Content Operator
Owns:
- growth surfaces
- marketing support
- product communication assets
- campaign or content alignment with product positioning

---

## Hiring Evaluation Criteria

Strong candidates should be evaluated on:
- clarity of thinking
- execution quality
- ability to work with structure
- understanding of product context
- ability to improve rather than complicate systems

For early-stage team building, attitude and systems thinking often matter as much as raw technical skill.

---

## Team Culture Goal

The long-term goal is to build a team that feels:
- serious,
- thoughtful,
- quality-driven,
- and comfortable operating in an iterative startup environment.
